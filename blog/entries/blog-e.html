And here is my poem:

Breath begins to fill my lungs and my brain feels clearer now.
I’m starting to really care for my own being
Instead of being wrapped up in how charming you are when you look at me.
Now I look at myself and see through you.
Beautiful.


Not in appearance,
but in the way a blue sky is beautiful.
You are clouds,
Grey and White hide the bright blue sky from view.
Baffling, but it is always there, even when I am blinded.
There the way oxygen is always there to fill my body with calm,
and presence.


It is beautiful to me,
That although you view me as silly
I see the sincere effort,
And smile still.
I know your porcelain shines through only for the moment
The sky is grey
Still, I gladly open my blinds to get a glance of you.


How silly of me, to fall under the impression that your smile and sparkling brown eyes see into me
and past yourself,
a cloud.
Some days I sulk in skies of white.
Staring at sheets of Strato-form
Thinking of your face
Looking forward to the day that my favorite color peeks through again
To appreciate the bright blue behind you
And the sun.


And I stare.
And stare.
And stare, while I still can.


You ought to be thanking me.
But I will thank you instead,
Thank you clouds.

I am starting to see the sun
And the blue behind you, for more than a moment.
And I smile.
